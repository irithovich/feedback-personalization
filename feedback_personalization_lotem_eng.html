  • Feedback Personalization

  • This tool will help you analyze the gaps between you and one of your employees, and choose coping strategies for managing the gaps effectively.

    Please follow the instructions:

    1. Think about one of your employees.

    2. Rate yourself and him/her on the different scales.

    3. Decide on practical next steps.

    Let's begin

  • Psychological Confidence

    • What is psychological confidence?

    • The confidence to learn, contribute, err and challenge the status quo without fear of judgment, embarrassment or formal or informal punishment for their behavior.

      People with high levels of self confidence feel appreciated by their manager and coworkers. They feel free to express themselves, give criticism and think outside the box.

      People with low levels of self confidence feel different, very sensitive to hints of criticism or rejection, tend not to express themselves even if they have smart things to say and the ability to contribute

    •

  • Personal Orientation

    • What is personal orientation?

    • Tendency towards individualism or collectivism

      Individualist - a person whose basic focus is first and foremost on their own needs and achievements within the organization.

      Collectivist - a person focused on the team and the group they are affiliated with.

    •

  • Perception of Authority

    Hierarchical vs. Egalitarian
    • What is authority perception?

    • Perception of the manager as equal OR different.

      People with egalitarian perceptions perceive their manager as equal to them, with the key difference being each one’s position.

      People with hierarchical perceptions of authority perceive the manager to be different from them and entirely responsible for the team's and their own success.

      When rating yourself, please address your management style.

      When rating your employee, please address your employee’s expectations as you understand them.

    •

  • Communication style in negative situations

    • What is communication style in negative situations?

    • The needs and behavior in negative situations such as negative feedback, conflicts, tensions and misunderstandings.

      People who are direct in their communication style tend to express their needs and expectations directly in every situation.

      People who are indirect place immense emphasis on social harmony. They prefer to express and receive complex messages in an indirect manner.

  • Emotional expression

    • What is emotional expression?

    • The level of emotional expression in the workplace.

      Emotionally neutral people do not express emotions in the workplace. Their communication is functional and analytical.

      People that are emotionally expressive express emotions at their workplace, with their feelings serving as the basis for understanding situations and making decisions.
